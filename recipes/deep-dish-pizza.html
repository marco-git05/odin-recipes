Deep-Dish Pizza

Description

The Chicago deep-dish pizza is a staple and a must-have for all pizza lovers. The pizza itself is made differently than a traditional pizza, using corn oil rather than butter to give the crust its iconic crispy and buttery consistency. This unique take on pizza makes this recipe a standout in the world of pizza.

Ingredients

  • Active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • White sugar (1 1/2 teaspoons)
  • Warm water (1 1/8 cups)
  • All-purpose flour (3 cups)
  • Corn oil (1/2 cup)
  • Kosher salt (1 1/2 teaspoon)
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Dissolve yeast and sugar in a bowl of warm water. Let stand until the yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam for about 5 to 10 minutes.
  2. Combine the yeast mixture, flour, corn oil, and kosher salt into a large stand mixer and knead until the dough olds together but still slightly sticky for about 2 minutes.
  3. Form the dought into a ball and transfer to a bowl that has been buttered. Turn to coat the dough with butter, then cover the bowl with a towel an let rise in a warm place until it has doubled in volume for about 6 hours.
  4. Punch down dough and let rest for about 10 to 15 minutes. Press dough into a 10-inch deep-dish pan.
  5. Add cheese, desired toppings, and sauce, in that order.
  6. Bake the pizza in an oven preheated to 450 degrees F for about 30 minutes.
